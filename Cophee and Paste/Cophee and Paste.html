Cophee and Paste

Have you ever had trouble deciding what dessert goes well with coffee? How about coffee as dessert? If that sounds interesting to you, then we believe we have the perfect drink for you: Cophee and Paste, which is our take on the Espresso con panna, shown below:

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons Whipping cream
  • 50ml Long or Short shot of espresso
  • (Optional) 1 teaspoon Vanilla syrup

Instructions

  1. Pull your espresso shot
  2. (Optional) Add vanilla syrup to whipping cream
  3. Dollop whipped cream on top of espresso

Notes

If you whisk the cream by hand or by electric mixer, mix until it is light and fluffy. If you don't have vanilla syrup, it can be subsituted with other sweeteners like sugar, maple syrup, or honey.

The best whipped cream is made with heavy cream, but you may use low-fat cream as well.

The Espresso

We use only the highest quality espresso beans available. Made up of a blend of 100% Arabica beans from both Latin America and Asian-Pacific, our espresso has a rich and caramelly taste with a fuller body and robust bold flavours.

To create our Cophee and Paste, you may use any type of espresso shot; ristretto, regular, or long all work very well. The long -or luongo- shot increases the flow of water through the beans to create a lighter taste with more volume, while the short -or ristretto- shot does the opposite. By restricting the flow of water, it increases the pressure in which the water goes through the coffee grounds, and creates a stronger, bold taste with less volume.

Drinking

How you drink this is purely a matter of preference.

You may mix the espresso with the cream to create a sweet drink.

Alternatively, you can sip the espresso by tilting the cup, with the cream mostly sitting on the top. The cream will gradually mix into the drink as it melts, and the drink will get sweeter over time.

Our Cophee and Paste goes well with many different toppings, including cocoa powder, cinnamon powder, and vanilla powder.
